Is Mobile eCommerce at a Tipping point in India?

03 AUG 2013
Posted By John Snow
LinkedIn
Facebook
Twitter

With close to a Billion mobile connections, India is the second largest mobile market in the world. while the desktop internet user base is poised to grow to 300 million by 2016, close to 60% or 180 million are expected to be accessed internet only from mobile over the course of next 3-4 years.

Prof. Kartik Hosanagar from Wharton recently stated that " mobile is key to the next e-commerce boom in India, because if you take pure e-commerce — eliminating mobile — the reach is very limited at present." which clearly states that if eco-system factors like 3G, Regulations around mobile payments happens fast, there is little doubt that Mobile is the “Right Technology" that pushes eCommerce to next level.



Mukesh Bansal, co-founder and CEO of Myntra , India’s largest online fashion store indicated that currently 12% of overall revenues are coming from Mobile and they expect it to grow to 20-25% over the course of next one year, similar analogy is expressed by Phanindra Sama, co-founder and CEO of Redbus, India’s largest bus ticketing company stated that they expected 50% of the booking and revenue to come from mobile over the next three years.

The above statements clearly indicate that “Mobile Commerce is at a tipping point in India" and is set to rapidly explode in the next three years.

While there are many issues like fragmentation in mobile world, delay in 3G roll out lack of adequate infrastructure etc. most of the issues will be resolved as consumers leap frog from desktop to mobile internet.

We at MartMobi.com are truly excited with the recent news and series of events and strongly believe that “Mobile eCommerce is set of Explode in India" very soon.
